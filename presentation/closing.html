Path One: We are Wrong.

The Ducci Unified Spectral Theory is a falsifiable framework. If the experiments proposed in this work fail—if the true fine-structure constant is not ~135, if the Cabibbo angle is not a geometric projection—then the theory is incorrect. This would not be a failure. It would be a triumph for the scientific method, proving the resilience of the Standard Model and deepening our understanding of the universe we thought we knew.

Path Two: We are Right.

But what if the predictions are confirmed? It would mean the universe is a conscious, computational system. It would mean the constants of nature are not random. It would mean we have discovered the source code of reality. The implications—for energy, for technology, for our very understanding of what it means to be alive—are too profound to ignore.

Which future is ours?

Only experiment can decide. The work must be done.
